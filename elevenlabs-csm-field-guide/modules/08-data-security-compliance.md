# Module 8: Data Security and Compliance
## What the IT Administrator Needs to Hear — Before They Kill the Deal

---

### The Ambush Risk

CSMs don't lose deals at the Business Owner stage.
They lose them quietly at the IT Administrator stage —
two weeks after everyone else said yes.

The IT Administrator who goes quiet in a buying committee
meeting is not satisfied. They are building a list.
A list of questions they didn't get answers to.
A list they will take to their CISO.
A list that becomes a formal security review
that stalls the deal for sixty days.

The CSM who can speak compliance fluently — certifications,
data flows, access controls, retention policies —
is the CSM who keeps the deal alive through IT review.
The CSM who can't is the one who gets the
"we've decided to go in a different direction" email.

---

### How IT Administrators Think

Before walking into any meeting with an IT Administrator
present, the CSM needs to understand what this person
is actually evaluating.

The IT Administrator is not evaluating whether ElevenLabs
is a good product. That's the Business Owner's job.
The IT Administrator is evaluating whether ElevenLabs
is a defensible vendor — one they can explain and
justify to their security team, their compliance officer,
and their CISO if something goes wrong.

Their questions translate as:

*"Where does our data go?"*
→ "If there's a breach, am I able to explain exactly
what data was exposed and why it was there?"

*"Who can access it?"*
→ "Can I demonstrate that access was controlled
and audited?"

*"What certifications do you hold?"*
→ "Is this vendor audited against standards my
organization already accepts?"

*"What happens when we leave?"*
→ "Can I prove our data is gone?"

Every answer the CSM gives needs to satisfy the person
behind the question — not just the question itself.

*When to cite it:* When the IT Administrator asks

**ISO 27001:2022**

*What the IT Administrator hears:* ElevenLabs operates
an Information Security Management System (ISMS)
that meets the current international standard.
This is the baseline enterprise security certification.

*When to cite it:* When the IT Administrator asks
"are you ISO certified?" — which is often the first
security question in international enterprise accounts.

---

**ISO 27018:2019**

*What the IT Administrator hears:* ElevenLabs has
been certified against the international standard
specifically for protection of personally identifiable
information (PII) in public cloud services.

*When to cite it:* When the IT Administrator or Legal
asks about voice data — specifically whether voice
recordings and clones are treated as PII.

*CSM talking point:*
> "ISO 27018 specifically covers PII protection in cloud
> environments. For voice data — which many privacy
> frameworks treat as biometric PII — this certification
> is the relevant one to pull up for your compliance team."

---

**ISO 27701:2019**

*What the IT Administrator hears:* ElevenLabs has
extended their ISMS to include privacy information
management — covering GDPR and other privacy regulations.

*When to cite it:* When the IT Administrator or Legal
asks about GDPR compliance or privacy program maturity.

**HIPAA**

*What the IT Administrator hears:* ElevenLabs has

not abstract. They are operational and auditable.

### What the CSM Answers — and What They Don't

**The CSM answers with confidence:**
- Which certifications ElevenLabs holds
- What each certification covers
- Where to find documentation (Trust Center)
- How to request access to gated compliance documents
- What controls are confirmed in the Trust Center

**The CSM escalates immediately:**
- Custom data residency requirements for specific jurisdictions
- Contractual data processing agreements (DPAs)
- SIEM integration or security monitoring requirements
- Specific data retention periods beyond "deleted upon departure"
- Business Associate Agreements (BAAs) for healthcare
- Any question that begins "but what if..." and ends
  in a scenario the CSM hasn't prepared for

**The escalation path:**
> "That's a question that needs your ElevenLabs account
> contact and potentially their technical and legal team
> involved. The Trust Center gives you the documented
> baseline — for anything beyond that baseline,
> let's get the right people in the conversation
> before you take this to your CISO."

The CSM who knows exactly where their knowledge ends
is the CSM the IT Administrator trusts.
