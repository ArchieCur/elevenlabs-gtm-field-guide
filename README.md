# ElevenLabs CSM Field Guide
## TTS and Voice Cloning — Scale and Business Tier

---

## Why This Guide Exists

This guide was designed to enhance your own skills as a CSM.
Hopefully it will provide a resource that helps you understand
the decision makers, their potential questions, and gives you
strategies to develop a strong working relationship.

This is not a script. It is not a product manual.
It is not a list of features to recite.

It is a practitioner survival guide for the buying conversation
you are actually going to have — with three people in the room
who speak three different languages, asking questions that
didn't appear in your onboarding deck, about a platform
that moves faster than any documentation can follow.

The CSM who walks in knowing what the IT Administrator's
silence means, what the Business Leader needs to defend
the decision upward, and what the Business Owner is afraid
their team won't be able to use — that CSM doesn't need
a script. They need preparation. That's what this guide provides.

---

## What This Guide Covers

**Platform:** ElevenLabs
**Scope:** Text to Speech (TTS) and Voice Cloning
**Buyer tier:** Scale and Business (verify current pricing
at elevenlabs.io/pricing before every buyer conversation)
**Audience:** CSMs and AEs working the expansion revenue motion

This guide does not cover ElevenAgents, AI Music generation,
the free or Creator tier buyer conversation, or deep API
implementation. Those are separate buying conversations
with separate technical and compliance profiles.

## Delivering the Guide — Onboarding Formats

The week-one onboarding sequence in Module 04 describes
what to cover with a new customer. Here is how to deliver it
when in-person isn't available:

**Sessions 1 and 2** (platform orientation and slider calibration)
can be delivered as a leave-behind video or quick start guide.
Record once, use with every new customer at this tier.

**Session 3** (voice consistency and asset protection)
works best as a live 30-minute call — in person or virtual.
This is where the CSM earns the relationship, not just
transfers the information.

**Recommended follow-up email after leaving the quick start:**

> *"Hey — did you get a chance to look at the quick start?
> When can we grab 30 minutes so I can show you the protips
> for maintaining voice consistency and protecting your assets?"*

This email does three things: follows up without pressure,
signals more value is coming, and reframes voice consistency
as something the customer owns and should care about —
not just a technical setting.

## How to Maintain This Guide

This guide is a snapshot. ElevenLabs moves fast.

**What changes frequently:**
- Pricing (verify at elevenlabs.io/pricing before every meeting)
- Integration statuses (Module 03 framework is stable;
  specific platform statuses may change)
- Trust Center certifications (verify at compliance.elevenlabs.io)
- Model availability and recommendations (Module 04)

**What changes slowly:**
- Buying committee dynamics (Module 06)
- Legal awareness framework (Module 02B)
- Platform architecture (Module 01)
- The honest limitations philosophy (Module 07)

**The maintenance protocol:**

When any platform fact in this guide changes —
pricing, integration status, certification, feature —
update the affected module and document the change
in the module's footer with the date verified.
Do not let module footers run more than 90 days
without a verification pass on time-sensitive content.

**A note on why this guide is built on frameworks
rather than specific numbers:**

During the drafting of Module 01, ElevenLabs updated
Business tier pricing from $1,100/mo to $1,320/mo
within a 24-hour window. The module was being written
at the time. This is why the guide states frameworks
and directs CSMs to verify current figures — not because
the numbers don't matter, but because they change faster
than any guide can follow.

The framework stays true. The numbers need checking.

## The Lab Notebook Protocol

Every decision in this guide is documented.

Every module has a decision notes file that records
what was decided, why it was decided, and who confirmed it.
Every review has a ruling document that records what was
flagged, how it was resolved, and what was confirmed and kept.

This is not bureaucracy. It is the standard that patent
law established for a reason: undocumented decisions
are decisions waiting to be relitigated. When a future
maintainer asks "why did we say it this way?" — the
answer is in the decision trail.

The lab notebook knows, even when the posse doesn't. 😄
